  • Key financial considerations

    We have listed six main considerations for a couple with children planning to end their marriage or civil partnership in England and Wales.

    48% of divorces in 2011 were between couples with children.

    This should help you prepare your finances

    Legal
    Advice     Shared Bank
    Accounts     House Children Assets Pensions

    • Legal advice

      Legal advice costs can reach £15,000+ per person for more complicated cases. Average cost of a divorce is £1,300. Minimum cost of a divorce is £36.

      • As much as possible, try reaching agreements with your ex-partner on as many topics before involving legal professionals.
      • But don’t shy away from legal advice. Separation is a complex issue and you may want to involve professionals to help you decide how to divide your financial assets and commitments fairly – known as a financial settlement.

    • Shared bank accounts

      82% said they had a joint bank account.

      • Speak to your financial providers to ensure necessary payments are not missed and your credit rating is protected while the divorce process concludes.
      • Money in joint accounts is normally split on an equal basis regardless of who paid in what, but if in doubt seek professional advice.

    • House

      Divorce or separation is one of the top ten reasons why people sell their homes. 9% of all homes are sold because of divorce.

      • Your financial settlement on divorce might include an order on how to split the matrimonial home. If you decide to sell your home you have two choices – sell on the open market or to your ex-partner.
      • If you rent, you may have to try to arrange a new tenancy agreement with your landlord. It may prove difficult to alter your rental term, so inform your landlord of this likelihood as soon as possible.

    • Children

      There are 1.7 dependent children in the average family. Over 80% of children of separated parents live exclusively or mainly with their mother.

      90% of parents sort out post-separation childcare between themselves; only 10% go to court.

      • Parents of dependent children (mostly those aged 18 and under and still in full-time education) must ensure that their children’s wellbeing is provided for.
      • You don’t need to involve the courts or the Child Maintenance Service. Agreeing provision with your ex-partner can keep costs down, and can help the emotional wellbeing of your child; but you still have recourse to the CMS and in some circumstances the courts if this arrangement breaks down.

    • Assets

      43% of 45–64s have a household wealth of more than £500,000. One in four people admit to hiding assets from their partner during a divorce.

      • It is in your interests to seek to agree on a fair division of the assets you have built up during your marriage or civil partnership before involving legal professionals. The two biggest assets are often the matrimonial home and pensions.
      • If you have concerns about what your ex-partner may be doing with matrimonial assets, such as failing to disclose them fully, then seek professional advice as soon as you can.

    • Pensions

      Whether you or your partner are already receiving retirement income from your pension(s) or you are nearing that point, you should seek professional help to divide any pensions fairly between you.

      This is because alongside the matrimonial home, pensions are often the biggest assets to divide when you separate. There are three ways to divide pensions:

      • Sharing: you are awarded a share of your ex-partner’s pensions, which would either be transferred to a pension scheme of your choosing or transferred into a separate pension in your name within the existing scheme.
      • Attachment: you receive an agreed amount of your ex-partner’s net pension income, or a lump sum (or both), when it starts to pay out.
      • Offsetting: the value of any pension is offset against other assets, such as the value of the family home.

      The partner who may not have worked for some years while raising children can adopt their spouses National Insurance contributions to ensure they receive the maximum state pension entitlement. You should continue to look for ways to save into a pension once you have separated so you have enough to live on in retirement.
